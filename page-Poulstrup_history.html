Until the middle of the 20th century, the area appeared as extensive heathland, as a result of centuries of grazing and torture of the earth.

After the liberation in 1945, the area was in a sad state, then the German occupying forces had used the land as a training ground for tanks.

At the request of Aalborg's then postmaster, J. B. Nicolaisen, the municipality bought parts of the scenic hill area in 1946, including Poulstrup Lake.

The area quickly became one popular excursion destination, and in a former country estate furnished a tractor place according to a completely new concept, called "cafeteria".
